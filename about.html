The Musical Group

MANGO: She was a chef in a renowned restaurant, known by name across town for her onion soup. Her days were spent cooking and her nights were spent unleashing the turmoil that came from chopping onions day after day on her drumset at home. It was when she found Kango lounging in the dumpster behind her kitchen, strumming a cherry red electric guitar while taking a bite out of a leftover onion, that she knew that music was the right path for her.

KANGO: She was once a simple onion farmer. Ever since she was young, she dreamed of becoming something more than her family’s traditional onion harvesting. And so she took to the city, taking nothing with her but the cherry red guitar she bought after selling leftover onions from her family’s farm. She was lost and alone and found herself in the back of a restaurant searching for a taste of home, but instead found family.

GANGO: Kango and Mango were taking a walk when they saw someone sitting on the ground, petting a dog and singing to it. They wanted to pet the dog too, so they joined her. Soon they found out that this was Gango, who was on the run for stealing three metric tons of onions. They agreed to take her under their wing and provide for her, so long as she kept singing. And so she only sung and never spoke.

RANGO: It was while Mango, Kango, and Gango were enjoying some of Mango’s world-class onion soup outside in a park when they heard the sound of a sad pianist. It wasn’t the piano playing; it was her wailing in despair. She just saw a deer that had been hit by a car. The three went to go comfort her with onion soup, and soon they found that their intuition had been correct. She was a sad pianist. And so the band was formed.
