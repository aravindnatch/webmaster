Who are We?

We are Lambert High School’s TSA Chapter, competing in the school’s fifth TSA season. Throughout the years, our program has been steadily increasing in the number of members and as an overall chapter. We have risen from the fifth outstanding chapter in the state to the 4th outstanding chapter last year. Our program has drastically changed in the way it’s being organized; more meetings are available to learn more information and workshops have been introduced to help the members find time to work on their projects. This chapter is also most notable for consistently placing in the top 10 for more than half of the available events.

Lambert Engineering

The first year course, designed to introduce students to many different aspects of engineering and build the fundamentals they’ll need. The second year course, Concepts, allows students to continue working on their 3D modeling skills while introducing more of the equipment in the shop. The third year course, Applications, is more open-ended, with students choosing several larger projects to work on that incorporate their skills and interests.

Lambert Engineering is fortunate to have a brand new classroom/lab space (renovated Summer 2016) which serves as the computer lab, basic workspace, home of multiple 3D printers, and storage for engineering activities. There is also a complete shop just down the hall containing a wide variety of equipment specifically for the engineering classes, ranging from a laser cutter and plasma cutter to a CNC mill, CNC lathe, and CNC router. Students have the opportunity, over the course of their time in engineering, to learn to operate different pieces of specialized equipment.

Stephanie VanSlyke

svanslyke@forsyth.k12.us

I began teaching 14 years ago after working in the business world (Ernst & Young). Somehow my high school math teacher always knew I would end up being a math teacher. I taught math in middle school for 9 years and moved up to Lambert High School in July 2015. I began teaching AP Computer Science A last year and absolutely love it now. I got involved with TSA to see the other things students do outside of class. I wanted to see the growth of each student and have a mentor relationship with them outside of the classroom.


Joe Fuqua

jfuqua@forsyth.k12.ga.us

My name is Joe Fuqua, I have a degree in electrical engineering; currently I am an engineering teacher at Lambert High School, where I teach high schoolers the basics of engineering to set them up for a future career in the profession. One of the best aspects of my job is interacting with students and inspiring them to do their best while having fun. Engineering to me is an opportunity for one to express themselves by creating, designing, and communicating with others. TSA is for those who strives to be the best in what they do, and I’m very proud to be part of this great opportunity.
