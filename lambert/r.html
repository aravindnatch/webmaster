Attendance & Checkpoints

 Before the start of every club meeting, each student must sign in to attendance onto a sheet of paper, which then gets uploaded to a google sheets form. During the weekly after school meetings, the officers explain the upcoming plan of action that is presentable to the rest of the group. This could be the announcement of a new workshop date, or the general information for a new conference. Also, these meetings provide an opportunity for students to get checked on their progress for their events by the officers and the advisors.

Community



Google Code-In

 During the months of November and December, this chapter's students are informed of the Google Code-In competition. In this competition, students are tasked with helping real Open-Source Companies. Winners of this competition receive a trip to Google Headquarters, and students who complete at least 3 tasks receive a t-shirt.

Women Who Code

Women Who Code is an international non-profit organization that provides services for women pursuing technology careers and a job board for companies seeking coding professionals. The company aims to provide an avenue into the technology world by evaluating and assisting women developing technical skills. Students in our program are encouraged to participate in this program.
